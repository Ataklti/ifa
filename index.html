Jimma

Jimma, renowned as the birthplace of Arabica coffee, has a fascinating legend associated with its discovery. As per Ethiopian folklore, a goat herder named Kaldi noticed his goats becoming lively after consuming berries from a specific tree in the Choche Ketta Mudouga area of the Gomma district in Jimma zone. Intrigued by this observation, Kaldi decided to taste the berries himself and experienced a sudden boost of energy. Jimma, alternatively spelled as Jima, is a town situated in southwestern Ethiopia, approximately 220 miles (353 km) southwest of Addis Ababa. Positioned at an elevation of 5,740 feet (1,750 meters), the town is enveloped by lush forests that are home to extensive coffee plantations. Serving as the commercial center of the region, Jimma is renowned for its coffee processing facilities and the production of various local goods.

Sidama

Sidamo coffee beans are internationally recognized for their exceptional quality, known for their floral scent & mild acidity. Cultivated at high altitudes, these beans mature slowly, absorbing a variety of rich flavors. The outcome is a sophisticated, strong coffee that offers a gratifying sensory experience, often demanding a higher price. The fertile soil & ideal climate of the region play a significant role in producing outstanding coffee beans. The combination of high elevation, rainfall, & cool temperatures creates a perfect environment for the beans to thrive, while careful harvesting and processing methods further enhance their quality. It is famous for its lively acidity, floral fragrances, & subtle notes of citrus & berries. With a medium body and smooth texture, it is admired for its balanced flavor profile. Highly sought after by coffee enthusiasts & specialty roasters worldwide, Sidamo coffee is usually roasted to bring out its full potential. Whether enjoyed as a pour-over, espresso, or cold brew, Sidamo coffee stands out with its exceptional taste and quality.
